                - \left(\sum_{i=0}^{107}p_i\right)^2
                = \mathbf{p}^{\top}(A-\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^{\top})\mathbf{p}
                  + \mathbf{b}^{\top}\mathbf{p},
\]

where \(\mathbf{1}\) is the all‑ones vector.  
Because \(A\) is positive‑definite but \(\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^{\top}\) has eigenvalue \(108\) in the
direction of \(\mathbf{1}\), the matrix \(A-\mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}^{\top}\) is
indefinite: it has at least one negative eigenvalue.  
Consequently the quadratic part can be made arbitrarily negative by increasing
the magnitude of \(\sum p_i\).  
The linear term \(\mathbf{b}^{\top}\mathbf{p}\) is negligible compared with the
quadratic term when the magnitudes of \(p_i\) are large.

Thus the function is unbounded below; the best achievable value is
\(-\infty\).  
To drive the objective toward large negative values we simply choose a large
constant value for every component, e.g. \(-10\).  
This keeps the sum \(\sum p_i\) large and negative, maximising the
negative \(-(\sum p_i)^2\) contribution while the positive part
\(\mathbf{p}^{\top}A\mathbf{p}\) grows only linearly in that constant.
